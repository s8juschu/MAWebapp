General information

The following study is part of a master thesis at the Deutschen Forschungszentrum für Künstliche Intelligenz (DFKI) and Universität des Saarlandes (UdS).

Voluntary participation and consent

Participation in the study is voluntary. Consent to participate in this study can be revoked at any time and without giving reasons until the end of the data collection by ending the survey (a corresponding button is available on the website) or closing the browser window. All incomplete data records will be deleted by us. After the end of the data collection, the legal rights of revocation, information, correction, blocking and deletion can no longer be exercised, because the data can no longer be assigned to the data subject.

What data do we collect?

After consenting to the data collection, the following data will be collected and processed anonymously only:
  • Input in the online survey

For technical reasons, the following data, among other things, which are transmitted from the Internet browser to us or to our web space provider, are recorded (so-called server log files):
  • Browser type and version
  • Operating system used
  • Website previously visited (referrer URL)
  • Date and time of access
  • Internet Protocol (IP) address

This anonymous data is not merged with the survey data. On this website we use so-called cookies. Cookies are small files that are stored by your web browser. The cookies used on our website do not cause any harm to your computer and do not contain any malware. They enable a user-friendly and effective use of this website. We use permanent cookies with a maximum validity period of 365 days. We use these cookies exclusively to exclude multiple participation.

Anonymization of data

With the transmission of the collected data to our server, an anonymization takes place, which makes an allocation of the collected data to a person practically impossible, if no personal data are entered in free text fields.

What do we use the collected data for?

The results and data will be used exclusively for scientific research purposes and they will be published in anonymous form as part of the final thesis and as a scientific publication of this study. Furthermore, they can be further processed in anonymous form for other scientific research purposes.

Clarification of Study Goal
The purpose of this post-study information page is to provide additional details about the study you just participated in. In this study, we used partial disclosure and deception. At the beginning, you were told that the purpose of this study was to investigate whether your cognitive abilities matched your experience while completing the tasks. However, this was only partially true. We also wanted to investigate whether framing had an impact on perceived competence. The framing effect describes that our decisions are influenced by the way information is presented. Therefore, each participant was randomly assigned to one of three groups: Either they were told after the first 8 tasks and the first questionnaire that their performance has exceeded our expectations or was not what we expected, or they were given a neutral sentence asking them to continue the study.

{% if framing == 0 %} You were in the control condition. This means that you were presented with the neutral statement: "Thank you for your answers so far. Please proceed with the survey.". {% endif %} {% if framing == 1 %} You were in the positive framing condition. This means that you were presented with the positive statement: "Very good, that’s an excellent beginning. In this round, your performance has exceeded our expectations. You’ll surely do well in the next round!" {% endif %} {% if framing == 2 %} You were in the negative framing condition. This means that you were presented with the negative statement: "Well, that’s a start. In this round, your performance was not what we expected. Hopefully you can do a little better in the next round!." {% endif %}

In reality, the framing condition you were presented with did not reflect your performance and was purely random. In fact, you answered out of 8 tasks correctly in the first round. In the second round, you answered out of 8 tasks correctly. We could not give participants complete information about the study prior to their participation, as this could have influenced their behavior during the study in a way that would have invalidated investigations of the research question. The reason for the deception in this study was to keep participants' behaviors and attitudes unaffected by the goals of the study.

Since some elements of the study are different from what was originally explained, there is another consent form for you to read and complete, if you are willing to allow us to use the information you have provided. This consent is also a record that the full purpose of the study was explained to you.

We really appreciate your participation and hope that this has been an interesting experience for you. Once all the data are collected and analyzed for this project, we plan on sharing this information with the research community through seminars, conferences, presentations, and journal articles.

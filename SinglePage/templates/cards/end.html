Clarification of Study Goal
{# Only display if completed study#} {# {% if page_nr == overall_count %}#} In this study, we used partial disclosure and deception. At the beginning, you were told that the purpose of this study was to investigate whether your cognitive abilities matched your experience while completing the tasks. However, this was only partially true. We also wanted to investigate whether framing had an impact on perceived competence. The framing effect describes that our decisions are influenced by the way information is presented. Therefore, each participant was randomly assigned to one of three groups: Either they were told after the first 8 tasks and the first questionnaire that they had performed well or poorly, or they were given a neutral sentence asking them to continue the study.

{% if framing == 0 %} You were in the control condition. This means that you were presented with the neutral statement: "Thank you for your answers so far. Please proceed with the survey.". {% endif %} {% if framing == 1 %} You were in the positive framing condition. This means that you were presented with the positive statement: "Thank you for your answers so far. You performed well in the first round. If you continue like this, you will perform better than most participants. Please proceed with the survey.". {% endif %} {% if framing == 2 %} You were in the negative framing condition. This means that you were presented with the negative statement: "Thank you for your answers so far. You performed poorly in the first round. If you continue like this, you will perform worse than most participants. Please proceed with the survey." {% endif %}

In reality, the framing condition you were presented with did not reflect your performance and was purely random. We could not give participants complete information about the study prior to their participation, as this could have influenced their behavior during the study in a way that would have invalidated investigations of the research question. The reason for the deception in this study was to keep participants' behaviors and attitudes unaffected by the goals of the study.
